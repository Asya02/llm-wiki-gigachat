# Preliminary spectrum analysis of the Aurora Signal

Prepared by: Dr. Mina Okoye, Remote Signal Lab

## Summary

The Aurora Signal contains three stable tones centered near 410 Hz, 630 Hz, and 990 Hz. The repeat interval is approximately 47 seconds, with drift under 0.8 seconds in the cleanest recordings.

## Findings

1. The signal is not consistent with ordinary broadband auroral hiss. It has a narrowband structure and a repeating service-pattern-like cadence.
2. The signal is also not well explained by a simple damaged cable model. A cable could act as a conductor or antenna, but the tone spacing looks intentionally engineered.
3. Recordings from Kestrel Cove show stronger amplitude during high tide than during high aurora-index periods.
4. The 410/630/990 Hz pattern could plausibly match a maintenance beacon sequence if converted through degraded electromechanical components.
5. The lab cannot prove that K-17 is the source without physical inspection of the old mechanism.

## Recommended wording

Use: "The most likely explanation is an old service beacon interacting with tidal and ice conditions."

Avoid: "The signal was caused by the aurora alone."

Avoid: "The K-17 mechanism has been fully verified as operational." That claim requires direct inspection.
