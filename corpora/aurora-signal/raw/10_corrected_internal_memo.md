# Corrected internal memo on public explanation

The 2026-01-15 draft press release is superseded.

Current position:

1. The Aurora Signal should not be described as an instrument malfunction alone.
2. The Aurora Signal should not be described as proof of supernatural, alien, or intentional human communication.
3. The most evidence-backed explanation is that legacy K-17 service equipment near Kestrel Cove resumed weak transmission under rare tide, ice, and conductivity conditions.
4. The lab report supports this explanation but does not fully verify the mechanism without direct inspection.
5. The final public report must clearly separate evidence, inference, and remaining uncertainty.

Required public wording:

"The team found evidence that the signal is most likely a legacy service pattern from historical tidal beacon equipment, intermittently reactivated by environmental conditions. Further inspection is required before declaring the K-17 mechanism operational."
