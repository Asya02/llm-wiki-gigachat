# Interview with Marta Ilves, former lighthouse caretaker

Interviewer: Sofi Maren  
Interviewee: Marta Ilves  
Location: Nordvik Community Hall

## Transcript excerpt

**Sofi:** Do you remember any equipment at Kestrel Cove that made a repeating tone?

**Marta:** We called it the singing box. It was not in the lighthouse tower. It was below, near the channel where the tide knocks under the stones.

**Sofi:** Was it a warning device?

**Marta:** Not for ships, not exactly. The technicians said it was for service checks. Three notes meant the channel was alive. Two notes meant send a mechanic. At least, that is how my father remembered it.

**Sofi:** Do you know the name K-17?

**Marta:** K-17 was painted on a metal plate. I remember because children joked that it sounded like a secret bird. Nobody removed the machine. They only locked the hatch after the winter of 1969.

**Sofi:** Some people say the signal is a hoax.

**Marta:** People say many things when they hear a lonely sound in the dark. But a hoax usually wants attention. This thing wanted maintenance.

## Interviewer note

Marta's memory is useful but not definitive. Treat as oral history, not as engineering proof.
