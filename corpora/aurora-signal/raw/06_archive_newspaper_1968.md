# Nordvik Gazette clipping, 1968

## New tidal echo beacon installed at Kestrel Cove

The Harbor Authority has completed installation of the K-17 tidal echo beacon beneath the old lighthouse foundation at Kestrel Cove.

The device is described as a maintenance aid for winter service crews. It listens to water movement in the channel and emits a coded service pattern when the channel is passable. Engineers say the device uses a mechanical tide-driven generator and a low-power service channel.

The article lists a three-tone test pattern for service crews, but the printed numbers are partially damaged. The legible section appears to read:

- low tone near 400 Hz
- middle tone near 600 Hz
- high tone near 1000 Hz

The clipping also mentions that the system was expected to be retired if a permanent cable line reached Pier Delta.

Margin note in blue pencil, likely added later:

"Locked after 1969 winter. Do not remove until channel survey complete."
