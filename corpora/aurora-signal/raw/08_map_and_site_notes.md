# Map and site relationship notes

This source describes relative site locations. Coordinates are fictional and only for internal mapping.

## Places

- **North Fox Station**: active weather station used by Lina and Timur. Base camp and main receiver location.
- **Kestrel Cove**: small inlet where the Aurora Signal is strongest during high tide.
- **Old Lighthouse Foundation**: ruined foundation above the cove; contains the locked iron hatch.
- **South Tidal Channel**: narrow channel that feeds water under the lighthouse foundation. Ice closure here coincided with the signal disappearing.
- **Pier Delta**: old service pier. Earlier cable maps show a trench from Pier Delta toward the cove, but measurements near the pier were weak.
- **Sand Spit**: low ridge near Kestrel Cove where the K-17 brass plate and ceramic insulator fragment were found.

## Distances

- North Fox Station to Kestrel Cove: 3.2 km
- Kestrel Cove to Old Lighthouse Foundation: 180 m uphill
- Old Lighthouse Foundation to South Tidal Channel: 90 m down through basalt steps
- Kestrel Cove to Pier Delta: 1.1 km along shore
- Sand Spit to Old Lighthouse Foundation: 260 m

## Site relationship summary

The strongest signal locations cluster around Kestrel Cove, South Tidal Channel, and the Old Lighthouse Foundation. The cable hypothesis points toward Pier Delta, but the measured signal near Pier Delta is weaker than expected.
